What is a Ward?

In Massachusetts, a “ward” is an official geographic unit (similar to a “county”) within a city or town to help officials manage elections.

Massachusetts Democratic Ward Committees
  • For the Massachusetts Democratic party, people who live within the same ward (or town) come together to promote the values, candidates, and priorities of the state (and national) Democratic Party to their neighbors — this group is called the Ward (or Town) Committee, and is elected every 4 years on Democratic voting ballots
  • At the annual Massachusetts State Democratic Party’s conventions (which are just large events), people nominated by the Ward or Town Committees provide inputs and opinions about statewide policy priorities, and approve candidates to be listed on the ballot as “Democrat”
  • The process to nominate people as delegates to the statewide convention is known as a “convention caucus”

You can learn more about the purpose and power of a Democratic Ward Committee from an interview on East Boston’s Zumix radio show

Boston Ward 10 Democratic Committee

Wards are not always drawn along neighborhood boundaries within cities and towns; Boston’s Ward 10 includes parts of Mission Hill, Roxbury, and Jamaica Plain.

Boston’s Ward 10 meets regularly to discuss important issues with local elected officials, and hosts events to educate the public about upcoming elections.

Everyone is welcome to join public Ward 10 events. You can attend and be nominated as a delegate to the state convention even if you are not a voting member of the Ward Committee.
